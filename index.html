The best way to compute the moment of inertia of such shapes is to create a mesh that covers the shape of interest, and calculate the moment of inertia for each one of the triangles that make up the mesh. The sum of all these individual moments will then give us the total value of the moment of inertia of the given shape. Two-dimensional meshes may be made up of squares or triangles, but I chose to use the triangular mesh since it is easier to generate for arbitrary geometries using third party mesh-generation tools.

Rubber Band Powered Car

This project was part of the MAE 94 - Computed Aided Design and Drafting class here at UCLA, a class devoted to teaching students how to CAD using SolidWorks. For this project, we had to form teams of three students, and each team had to design and 3D print a vehicle that can move using only three rubber bands. The image above shows the design that my team came up with. The third rubber band was cut in half and used around the wheels to increase friction with the floor.

This image shows the engineering drawing of the main body of our vehicle. We had to create such drawings for all the parts of the car.

This image shows an exploded view of the assembly of the car, used to demonstrate the different parts of the vehicle.

An important part of this project was making sure that our vehicle could withstand the forces applied to it through the rubber bands. In order to do that we performed an FEA analysis using Solidworks to see which parts would be under the most stress and what displacement would that stress cause. To quantify the force exerted by the rubber band, we first computed the elastic constant k of the rubber bands and then calculated the maximum force it could apply based on how far we could displace it from its equilibrium position without breaking it.

This image shows the stress distribution on the main body of the car, caused by the rubber bands.

This image shows the displacement analysis results on the main body of the car, caused by the forces exerted by the rubber bands.

This image shows the final 3D printed vehicle.

This project was undoubtedly my favorite so far, because of the detailed documentation, the different skills it helped me develop, and the amount of collaboration and team communcation it required.

Internship at Motor Oil Hellas

During the summer of 2014 I worked for Motor Oil Hellas, a leading oil company focusing on oil refining and trading. My main responsibility was to analyze petrochemical and automotive industry reports, for energy trends and technological innovations in the mobility sector until 2050. Later on the findings of my research were included in a presentation made to the president of the company.

The analysis focuses on how the technological innovations in the automotive industry will affect the energy demand around the world. A very interesting observation that I made was that each company's projections where undeniably in favor of their industry. More specifically, petroleum companies' future projections show that at least 90% of the worlds' cars will run on gas until 2050, whereas electric vehicle companies suggest that electric cars will account for the biggest part of the global car market.

This image shows an interesting graph that represents a projection of the way the global car fleet will change until 2050.

The conclusions of my analysis were the following:

  1. Energy:
    • Population increase leads to an increase in energy demand, as well as an overpopulation of the big cities, having implications on community patterns and energy consumption.
    • Energy growth is mainly focused to non OECD countries.
    • Most energy is consumed by the industry sector while the transport sector maintains a relatively low percentage.
    • Oil still seems to dominate the fuel market (especially in the transport sector), even though renewable forms of energy make a fair penetration into the market.
    • Coal and gas show an increase in demand mainly for industrial use
  2. Regulations:
    • Tighter measures and standards are being set to reduce GHG emissions both for road transport as well as fuel suppliers.
    • Regulations include also the marine sector and propose SOx and NOx emissions reductions.
    • Regulations and measures to increase alternative fuel infrastructure.
  3. Technology:
    • Engine improvements for better performance and lower emissions.
    • Plug-in hybrid vehicles seem to be the dominant type of car for the next decades.
    • Technological innovations make alternative fueled cars realistic options.
    • Technological innovations in materials used in car manufacturing improve even more the performance and quality of vehicles.
    • Informatics seem to be the future with ultimate goal the self-driving car.
  4. Mobility:
    • Will exert influence in the technological developments in big cities in the future.

This internship was my first experience in a real world working environment and it gave a very solid idea of how a large company works.
